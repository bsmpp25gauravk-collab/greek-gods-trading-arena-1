Δ

DELTA — The God of Direction

How much does my option price move when the stock moves ₹1?

What is Delta?

Delta (Δ) tells you how much your option's price changes when the underlying asset moves by ₹1. It is the most important Greek for day-to-day trading and the starting point for all risk management.

BSM FORMULA — Step 1: Calculate d1 and d2
d1 = [ ln(S/K) + (r + σ²/2) × T ] / (σ × √T)
d2 = d1 − σ × √T
S = Spot Price  |  K = Strike  |  T = Time in years  |  r = Risk-free rate  |  σ = Implied Volatility
Δ (Call) = N(d1)
Δ (Put) = N(d1) − 1
N() = Cumulative Standard Normal Distribution (≈ area under the bell curve up to d1)

Key Ranges

Deep OTM Call
≈ 0.05
ATM Call
≈ 0.50
Deep ITM Call
≈ 0.95
ATM Put
≈ −0.50

Practical Uses

🎯Hedge Ratio

Delta = 0.50 means to be delta-neutral, sell 0.50 × lot_size units of the underlying per option lot.

🎲Probability Proxy

ATM call delta 0.50 → ~50% chance of expiring ITM. OTM call delta 0.10 → ~10% chance.

📊Portfolio Direction

Portfolio Delta = Σ(position size × delta). A delta-neutral book has zero net directional exposure.

🇮🇳 NSE Nifty Example

Nifty at 23,741. You buy 1 lot (65 units) of 23,750 CE (ATM call). BSM gives d1 = 0.026, so Delta = N(0.026) ≈ 0.51.

If Nifty rises ₹100 → option gains ≈ ₹51/unit × 65 = +₹3,315. Dollar Delta = 0.51 × 23,741 × 65 = ₹7.9 lakh equivalent exposure.

⚡ Quick Facts

Call range0 to +1
Put range−1 to 0
ATM value≈ ±0.50
Key formulaN(d1)

⚠️ Key Risk

Delta changes constantly as spot moves. A delta-neutral position becomes directional after any move. This change rate is Gamma.

Γ

GAMMA — The God of Acceleration

How fast is my Delta changing?

What is Gamma?

Gamma (Γ) is the rate of change of Delta per ₹1 move in the underlying. If Delta is velocity, Gamma is acceleration. High Gamma means your P&L can swing violently with small moves.

BSM FORMULA
Γ = N'(d1) / (S × σ × √T)
N'(d1) = Standard Normal PDF = (1/√2π) × e^(−d1²/2)
Always POSITIVE for long options · Always NEGATIVE for short options
🚀Long Gamma = Convexity

Long options benefit more from big moves than Delta predicts. Gains exceed the linear delta estimate on large moves.

💥Short Gamma Danger

Option sellers have negative gamma. A gap move causes Delta to swing far beyond initial estimates — losses can be catastrophic.

📰 GameStop Gamma Squeeze (Jan 2021)

GME rose from $20 to $483. Short sellers had sold calls (negative gamma). As GME rose, they were forced to buy shares to delta-hedge, which pushed GME higher, requiring more buying. Gamma created the self-reinforcing feedback loop.

⚡ Quick Facts

Long options+Gamma
Short options−Gamma
Highest atATM + near expiry

⚠️ Key Risk

Bank Nifty gamma on expiry morning can be 20× normal levels. Short ATM positions near expiry carry extreme risk.

Θ

THETA — The God of Time

How much value does my option lose each day?

What is Theta?

Theta (Θ) measures daily time decay. For option buyers it is a silent daily cost; for sellers it is daily income. Theta accelerates sharply in the final week before expiry.

BSM FORMULA
Θ (Call) = −[S × N'(d1) × σ / (2√T)] − r × K × e^(−rT) × N(d2)
Daily Theta = Annual Theta / 365
Negative for long options (cost) · Positive for short options (income)
💰Sellers earn Theta

Short strangles, iron condors, covered calls all collect theta daily. Every calm day adds to P&L without any spot move.

⏳Buyers pay Theta

Long calls must predict direction AND timing. A correct directional view still loses money if the move is too slow.

⚡ Quick Facts

Long optionsNegative Θ
Short optionsPositive Θ
Fastest atFinal 7 DTE

⚠️ Key Risk

Theta sellers are short gamma. The theta income can be wiped out in minutes by a large gap move.

ν

VEGA — The God of Volatility

How much does my option move when IV changes by 1%?

What is Vega?

Vega (ν) measures how much your option's price changes when Implied Volatility (IV) moves by 1 percentage point. Vega affects P&L even when the underlying doesn't move — pure vol risk.

BSM FORMULA
ν = S × √T × N'(d1) (divide by 100 for per 1% IV move)
Always POSITIVE for long options · Always NEGATIVE for short options
📈IV Expansion

Before earnings/RBI, IV rises. Long options gain value purely from vega — even if the stock doesn't move.

📉IV Crush

After events resolve, IV collapses. A correct directional bet still loses money if IV drops more than spot moved.

📰 XIV Collapse — Volmageddon (Feb 2018)

XIV ETN was massively short vega. VIX doubled (17→37) in one session. XIV's rebalancing mechanism amplified losses — it sold VIX futures into a rising vol market. Product lost 96% in one day and was terminated.

⚡ Quick Facts

Long options+Vega
Short options−Vega
Highest atATM + long-dated

⚠️ Key Risk

Volatility can double in a single session. Always stress-test vega at +10 and +20 vol point shocks.

ρ

RHO — The God of Interest Rates

How does my option value change when interest rates change?

BSM FORMULA
ρ (Call) = K × T × e^(−rT) × N(d2) / 100
ρ (Put) = −K × T × e^(−rT) × N(−d2) / 100
Calls: positive Rho (benefit from rate rises) · Puts: negative Rho

Rho has minimal impact on short-dated NSE weekly options (T is tiny). It matters for 60+ DTE options or LEAPS strategies where a 50bps RBI rate change can meaningfully shift option values.

⚡ Quick Facts

CallsPositive ρ
PutsNegative ρ
Impact (weekly)Very low
Impact (LEAPS)Significant
Σ

PORTFOLIO GREEKS — The Full Picture

Individual Greeks aggregate — the sum is what really matters.

AGGREGATION FORMULAS
Portfolio Delta = Σ (Contracts × Lot Size × Delta_i)
Portfolio Gamma = Σ (Contracts × Lot Size × Gamma_i)
Portfolio Theta = Σ (Contracts × Lot Size × Theta_i) ← daily ₹ value
Portfolio Vega = Σ (Contracts × Lot Size × Vega_i) ← ₹ per 1% IV
Negative lots for short positions · Positive lots for long positions
DOLLAR GREEKS — RISK IN RUPEES
Dollar Delta = Portfolio Delta × Spot Price
Dollar Vega = Portfolio Vega (already in ₹ per 1% IV)
Dollar Theta = Portfolio Theta (already in ₹/day)

Second-order Greeks measure how the primary Greeks themselves change. They matter for large books, complex strategies, and precise delta-hedging. Knowing these separates intermediate traders from advanced practitioners.

∂Δ/∂σ

VANNA — Delta-Volatility Cross

How does Delta change when Implied Volatility changes?

DEFINITION
Vanna = ∂Δ / ∂σ = ∂ν / ∂S
Vanna = −N'(d1) × d2 / σ
Also = rate of change of Vega with respect to Spot

Vanna tells you how much your delta hedge will need to change if IV moves. If IV spikes 10 points, your delta changes not just from Gamma (spot move) but also from Vanna (vol move). Large vanna means delta hedges deteriorate exactly when volatility spikes — when you need them most.

📊Risk Desk Use

Large options desks monitor vanna to keep delta hedges accurate during vol events like RBI announcements or global risk-off moves.

💡Behaviour

Vanna is largest for OTM options where delta is most sensitive to vol changes. It approaches zero for deep ITM or deep OTM options.

⚡ Quick Facts

Also written∂²V / ∂S∂σ
Largest atOTM options
Long call signPositive
∂Δ/∂t

CHARM — Delta Decay

How does Delta change as time passes each day?

DEFINITION
Charm = ∂Δ / ∂t = ∂Θ / ∂S
Charm (Call) = −N'(d1) × [2rT − d2σ√T] / (2Tσ√T)
Also = rate of change of Theta with respect to Spot

Charm means your delta hedge goes stale overnight or over a weekend — even with zero spot movement. Near-ATM options approaching expiry have high charm. Professional desks adjust delta hedges at the open each morning to account for charm, before making any spot-based adjustments.

📅Weekend Risk

Over a 3-day weekend, charm can shift an ATM option's delta by 5–10 points — significant for a large delta-neutral book.

⏱Expiry Week

During Bank Nifty expiry week, charm accelerates. Delta hedge ratios must be updated multiple times per day even with no spot move.

⚡ Quick Facts

Also calledDelta Decay
Largest atNear-ATM, low DTE
Key useOvernight hedge adjustment
∂ν/∂σ

VOMMA — Vega Convexity

How does Vega change when Implied Volatility changes?

DEFINITION
Vomma = ∂ν / ∂σ = ∂²V / ∂σ²
Vomma = ν × d1 × d2 / σ
Also called Volga or Vega Convexity

Positive vomma means your vega increases when IV rises — you get more vega exposure exactly when vol is expanding. OTM options have high vomma because their vega is most sensitive to IV changes. In a crisis vol spike (like March 2020), positive vomma generates accelerating gains.

💥Crisis Behaviour

Positive vomma = accelerating gains during vol spikes. Each additional IV point adds more P&L than the last. Long strangles benefit here.

🎯Hedging Use

Traders seeking pure volatility exposure structure positions for maximum vomma — cost-effective vol hedging with leverage to large spikes.

⚡ Quick Facts

Also calledVolga
Highest forOTM options
Long optionsPositive vomma

⚠️ Key Risk

Short options have negative vomma. During a vol spike, losses from vega accelerate — the exact mechanism that destroyed XIV.

∂³V

COLOR, SPEED & ZOMMA

Third-order Greeks for institutional desks

🎨Color (∂Γ/∂t)

Rate of change of Gamma over time. Used to predict how gamma hedging needs shift as expiry approaches. Critical for Bank Nifty weekly desks.

⚡Speed (∂Γ/∂S)

Rate of change of Gamma with respect to spot. Tells you how gamma hedge requirements change as the stock moves. High speed = rapidly shifting gamma on large moves.

🔬Zomma (∂Γ/∂σ)

Rate of change of Gamma with respect to volatility. A gamma hedge accurate at current IV may become wrong if IV moves — zomma quantifies that risk.
